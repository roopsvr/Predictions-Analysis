📋 Methodology

How this analysis works and what the data represents

In Plain English: This report analyses 10 years of Adelaide property sales data to identify which suburbs have the strongest conditions for future capital growth. Every suburb is scored across 5 signals — value, momentum, demand, price velocity and consistency — then ranked within their own price tier so Entry suburbs compete against Entry suburbs only, not against Premium suburbs.
🔬 Data Pipeline & Technical Detail

Source: Adelaide Metro Median Housing Sales dataset — quarterly suburb medians from Land Services SA via Kaggle.

Cleaning steps: Removed 3,701 zero-median rows (quarters with no sales) → removed 6,001 rows with fewer than 3 sales (statistically unreliable medians) → capped medians at $8M (removes Gilberton outlier at $10.25M from 4 sales) → deduplicated suburb/quarter rows keeping highest sales count → recalculated growth using 4-quarter shift (original year_change column had impossible values like −178%) → removed rows where recalculated growth exceeded ±100%/200%.

Result: ~12,900 reliable records from 399 unique suburbs across 22 councils.

Scoring: All 5 signals normalised 0–20 using min-max scaling within each price tier. Composite = S1×0.30 + S2×0.25 + S3×0.25 + S4×0.15 + S5×0.05.
